# Ideals

## Definition

> [!info] Definition (Ideal)
> Let $R$ be a [[02 - Ring Theory/Concepts/Ring Definition|ring]]. A subset $I \subseteq R$ is an **ideal** (two-sided ideal) if:
>
> 1. $(I, +)$ is a [[01 - Group Theory/Concepts/Subgroups|subgroup]] of $(R, +)$
> 2. For all $r \in R$ and $a \in I$: $ra \in I$ and $ar \in I$

> [!tip] Ideal Test
> $I$ is an ideal if and only if:
> 1. $I \neq \emptyset$ (usually check $0 \in I$)
> 2. $a - b \in I$ for all $a, b \in I$
> 3. $ra, ar \in I$ for all $r \in R$, $a \in I$

## Types of Ideals

> [!info] Left Ideal
> $I$ is a **left ideal** if $ra \in I$ for all $r \in R$, $a \in I$.

> [!info] Right Ideal
> $I$ is a **right ideal** if $ar \in I$ for all $r \in R$, $a \in I$.

> [!info] Two-sided Ideal
> $I$ is a **two-sided ideal** if it's both left and right ideal.
> For commutative rings, all ideals are two-sided.

## Generated Ideals

> [!info] Principal Ideal
> The ideal **generated by** $a \in R$ is:
> $$\langle a \rangle = (a) = RaR = \{r_1 a s_1 + \cdots + r_n a s_n : r_i, s_i \in R\}$$
> For commutative rings: $(a) = aR = Ra = \{ra : r \in R\}$

> [!info] Ideal Generated by Set
> For $S \subseteq R$:
> $$\langle S \rangle = \bigcap_{I \supseteq S, I \text{ ideal}} I$$

## Prime and Maximal Ideals

> [!info] Prime Ideal
> An ideal $P \subsetneq R$ is **prime** if:
> $$ab \in P \Rightarrow a \in P \text{ or } b \in P$$

> [!info] Maximal Ideal
> An ideal $M \subsetneq R$ is **maximal** if there's no ideal $I$ with $M \subsetneq I \subsetneq R$.

> [!abstract] Key Characterizations
> For commutative ring $R$ with unity:
> - $P$ is prime $\iff$ $R/P$ is an integral domain
> - $M$ is maximal $\iff$ $R/M$ is a field
> - Maximal ideals are prime

## Examples

> [!example] Example 1: $n\mathbb{Z}$ in $\mathbb{Z}$
> - All ideals of $\mathbb{Z}$ are principal: $I = n\mathbb{Z}$ for some $n \geq 0$
> - Prime ideals: $(0)$ and $(p)$ for prime $p$
> - Maximal ideals: $(p)$ for prime $p$

> [!example] Example 2: $(x)$ in $\mathbb{R}[x]$
> - $(x) = \{f(x) \in \mathbb{R}[x] : f(0) = 0\}$
> - Maximal because $\mathbb{R}[x]/(x) \cong \mathbb{R}$ is a field

> [!example] Example 3: $(x, y)$ in $\mathbb{R}[x, y]$
> - $(x, y) = \{f \in \mathbb{R}[x, y] : f(0, 0) = 0\}$
> - Maximal because $\mathbb{R}[x, y]/(x, y) \cong \mathbb{R}$
> - **Not** principal

## Operations on Ideals

| Operation | Definition |
|-----------|------------|
| Sum | $I + J = \{a + b : a \in I, b \in J\}$ |
| Product | $IJ = \{\sum a_i b_i : a_i \in I, b_i \in J\}$ |
| Intersection | $I \cap J$ |
| Colon ideal | $(I : J) = \{r \in R : rJ \subseteq I\}$ |
