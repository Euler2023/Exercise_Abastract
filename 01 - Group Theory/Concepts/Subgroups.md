# Subgroups

## Definition

> [!info] Definition (Subgroup)
> Let $(G, \cdot)$ be a [[01 - Group Theory/Concepts/Group Definition|group]]. A non-empty subset $H \subseteq G$ is a **subgroup** of $G$, written $H \leq G$, if $(H, \cdot)$ is itself a group under the same operation.

## Subgroup Criterion

> [!tip] One-Step Subgroup Test
> $H \leq G$ if and only if $H \neq \emptyset$ and $\forall a, b \in H$, $ab^{-1} \in H$.

> [!tip] Two-Step Subgroup Test
> $H \leq G$ if and only if:
> 1. $H \neq \emptyset$
> 2. $\forall a, b \in H$, $ab \in H$ (closure)
> 3. $\forall a \in H$, $a^{-1} \in H$ (inverses)

## Key Properties

1. Every group $G$ has at least two subgroups: $\{e\}$ and $G$ (trivial subgroups)
2. The identity of $H$ is the same as the identity of $G$
3. Inverses in $H$ are the same as inverses in $G$
4. Intersection of subgroups is a subgroup: $H \cap K \leq G$ if $H, K \leq G$
5. Union of subgroups is generally **not** a subgroup

## Examples

> [!example] Example 1: $n\mathbb{Z} \leq \mathbb{Z}$
> The multiples of $n$ form a subgroup of $(\mathbb{Z}, +)$:
> - $n\mathbb{Z} = \{nk : k \in \mathbb{Z}\} = \{\ldots, -2n, -n, 0, n, 2n, \ldots\}$

> [!example] Example 2: $SL_n(F) \leq GL_n(F)$
> Matrices with determinant 1 form a subgroup of invertible matrices.

> [!example] Example 3: $A_n \leq S_n$
> Even permutations form a subgroup of the symmetric group.
> - $|A_n| = n!/2$

## Generated Subgroups

> [!info] Definition (Generated Subgroup)
> For $S \subseteq G$, the subgroup **generated by $S$** is:
> $$\langle S \rangle = \bigcap_{H \leq G, S \subseteq H} H$$
> This is the smallest subgroup containing $S$.

For a single element:
$$\langle a \rangle = \{a^n : n \in \mathbb{Z}\} = \{\ldots, a^{-2}, a^{-1}, e, a, a^2, \ldots\}$$
