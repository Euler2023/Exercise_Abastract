# Langlands Program

## Introduction

The **Langlands program** is a vast web of conjectures and theorems connecting two seemingly disparate areas of mathematics:
- **Galois representations** (number theory/arithmetic geometry)
- **Automorphic representations** (harmonic analysis/representation theory)

Proposed by Robert Langlands in a famous 1967 letter to André Weil, it has become one of the central organizing principles of modern mathematics.

## The Grand Vision

> [!abstract] Langlands Philosophy
> There should be a natural correspondence:
> $$\left\{\begin{array}{c}\text{Galois representations}\\ \rho: \text{Gal}(\overline{K}/K) \to {}^L G\end{array}\right\} \longleftrightarrow \left\{\begin{array}{c}\text{Automorphic representations}\\ \pi \text{ of } G(\mathbb{A}_K)\end{array}\right\}$$
>
> Moreover, this correspondence should match **L-functions**:
> $$L(s, \rho) = L(s, \pi)$$

## Key Objects

### Galois Side

| Object | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| $\text{Gal}(\overline{K}/K)$ | Absolute Galois group of number field $K$ |
| $\rho: \text{Gal} \to \text{GL}_n(\overline{\mathbb{Q}}_\ell)$ | $\ell$-adic Galois representation |
| ${}^L G$ | Langlands dual group (L-group) |
| $L(s, \rho)$ | Artin L-function |

### Automorphic Side

| Object | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| $G$ | Reductive algebraic group over $K$ |
| $\mathbb{A}_K$ | Adele ring of $K$ |
| $G(\mathbb{A}_K)$ | Adelic points of $G$ |
| $\pi$ | Automorphic representation |
| $L(s, \pi)$ | Automorphic L-function |

## The Correspondence

### For GL₁: Class Field Theory

> [!info] Classical Case
> For $G = \text{GL}_1$, the Langlands correspondence is **class field theory**:
> $$\text{Gal}(K^{ab}/K) \cong K^\times \backslash \mathbb{A}_K^\times$$
>
> - 1-dimensional Galois representations ↔ characters of the idele class group
> - This is the **Artin reciprocity law**

### For GL₂: Modularity

> [!abstract] Modularity Theorem (Wiles, Taylor-Wiles, BCDT)
> For $G = \text{GL}_2$ over $\mathbb{Q}$:
>
> Every elliptic curve $E/\mathbb{Q}$ is **modular**: there exists a newform $f$ such that
> $$L(E, s) = L(f, s)$$
>
> The Galois representation on $T_\ell(E)$ corresponds to the automorphic representation generated by $f$.

This was the key ingredient in the proof of **Fermat's Last Theorem**.

### For GLₙ: Harris-Taylor, Henniart

> [!abstract] Theorem (Local Langlands for GLₙ)
> The **local Langlands correspondence** for $\text{GL}_n$ over a local field is a theorem:
> $$\{\text{$n$-dim Weil-Deligne reps}\} \longleftrightarrow \{\text{Irreducible admissible reps of } \text{GL}_n(F)\}$$
>
> Proved by Harris-Taylor (2001) and Henniart (2000).

## Structure of the Program

### Local vs Global

```
                    Langlands Program
                          │
            ┌─────────────┴─────────────┐
            │                           │
      Local Langlands            Global Langlands
            │                           │
    ┌───────┴───────┐           ┌───────┴───────┐
    │               │           │               │
  p-adic        Archimedean   Number        Function
  fields         (ℝ, ℂ)       fields         fields
```

### Local Langlands

> [!info] Local Correspondence
> For a local field $F$ (e.g., $\mathbb{Q}_p$, $\mathbb{R}$):
> $$\{\text{Langlands parameters}\} \longleftrightarrow \{\text{Irreducible admissible reps of } G(F)\}$$

**Status:**
- $\text{GL}_n$: Proved (Harris-Taylor, Henniart)
- Classical groups: Proved (Arthur, 2013)
- General reductive: Known in many cases

### Global Langlands

> [!info] Global Correspondence
> For a number field $K$:
> $$\{\text{Motives / Galois reps}\} \longleftrightarrow \{\text{Automorphic reps of } G(\mathbb{A}_K)\}$$

**Status:** Largely conjectural, but many cases proved

## Key Conjectures

### Functoriality

> [!abstract] Langlands Functoriality Conjecture
> For any homomorphism of L-groups ${}^L H \to {}^L G$, there should be a **transfer** of automorphic representations:
> $$\pi_H \mapsto \pi_G$$
>
> preserving L-functions.

### Reciprocity

> [!abstract] Langlands Reciprocity Conjecture
> Every **motivic** Galois representation should be automorphic:
> - Comes from geometry (étale cohomology of varieties)
> - Corresponds to an automorphic representation

### Specific Instances

| Conjecture | Statement | Status |
|------------|-----------|--------|
| **Artin Conjecture** | Artin L-functions are entire | Open (partial results) |
| **Ramanujan Conjecture** | Bounds on Hecke eigenvalues | Known for $\text{GL}_2/\mathbb{Q}$ |
| **Sato-Tate** | Distribution of $a_p$ | Proved (2011) |
| **Fontaine-Mazur** | Geometric Galois reps are motivic | Open |

## Major Theorems

### Proved Cases

> [!success] Modularity of Elliptic Curves (Wiles et al.)
> Every elliptic curve over $\mathbb{Q}$ is modular.

> [!success] Serre's Modularity Conjecture (Khare-Wintenberger)
> Every odd, irreducible 2-dimensional mod $p$ Galois representation arises from a modular form.

> [!success] Sato-Tate Conjecture (Barnet-Lamb et al.)
> For a non-CM elliptic curve $E/\mathbb{Q}$, the normalized Frobenius traces $a_p/2\sqrt{p}$ are equidistributed according to the Sato-Tate measure $\frac{2}{\pi}\sqrt{1-t^2}\,dt$.

> [!success] Potential Modularity (Taylor et al.)
> Many Galois representations become automorphic after a finite base change.

## The L-Group

> [!info] Definition
> The **Langlands dual group** (or **L-group**) ${}^L G$ is:
> $${}^L G = \widehat{G} \rtimes \text{Gal}(\overline{K}/K)$$
>
> where $\widehat{G}$ is the **dual group** (root data with roots and coroots swapped).

### Examples

| $G$ | $\widehat{G}$ |
|-----|---------------|
| $\text{GL}_n$ | $\text{GL}_n$ (self-dual) |
| $\text{SL}_n$ | $\text{PGL}_n$ |
| $\text{Sp}_{2n}$ | $\text{SO}_{2n+1}$ |
| $\text{SO}_{2n+1}$ | $\text{Sp}_{2n}$ |
| $\text{SO}_{2n}$ | $\text{SO}_{2n}$ |

## Geometric Langlands

> [!info] Geometric Version
> Over a curve $X$ over $\mathbb{C}$ (or $\mathbb{F}_q$), replace:
> - Galois representations → local systems (flat connections)
> - Automorphic forms → D-modules on $\text{Bun}_G(X)$
>
> The **geometric Langlands correspondence** is an equivalence of derived categories.

This connects to:
- Mathematical physics (S-duality, mirror symmetry)
- Quantum field theory
- Geometric representation theory

## Applications

### Number Theory
- Proof of Fermat's Last Theorem (via modularity)
- Sato-Tate conjecture
- Cases of BSD conjecture

### Representation Theory
- Character formulas
- Branching rules
- Unitary dual problems

### Physics
- Gauge theory / S-duality
- Geometric Langlands ↔ Mirror symmetry
- Quantum field theory
