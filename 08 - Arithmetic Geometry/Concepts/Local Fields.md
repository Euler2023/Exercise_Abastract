# Local Fields

## Definition

> [!info] Definition (Local Field)
> A **local field** is a field $K$ that is complete with respect to a discrete valuation and has finite residue field.

Equivalently: a locally compact, non-discrete topological field.

## Classification

> [!abstract] Theorem (Classification of Local Fields)
> Every local field is isomorphic to one of:
> 1. **Characteristic 0**: Finite extension of $\mathbb{Q}_p$ (p-adic fields)
> 2. **Characteristic $p > 0$**: $\mathbb{F}_q((t))$ (formal Laurent series)

Also included (by convention):
- $\mathbb{R}$ and $\mathbb{C}$ (Archimedean local fields)

## Structure

### Ring of Integers

> [!info] Definition
> For a non-Archimedean local field $K$:
> - **Ring of integers**: $\mathcal{O}_K = \{x \in K : v(x) \geq 0\}$
> - **Maximal ideal**: $\mathfrak{m}_K = \{x \in K : v(x) > 0\}$
> - **Residue field**: $k = \mathcal{O}_K/\mathfrak{m}_K \cong \mathbb{F}_q$
> - **Uniformizer**: $\pi$ with $v(\pi) = 1$

### Key Properties

| Property | Formula |
|----------|---------|
| $\mathcal{O}_K$ | DVR, PID |
| $K^\times$ | $\cong \pi^\mathbb{Z} \times \mathcal{O}_K^\times$ |
| $\mathcal{O}_K^\times$ | $\cong \mu_{q-1} \times (1 + \mathfrak{m}_K)$ |
| $(1 + \mathfrak{m}_K)$ | Pro-$p$ group |

## Examples

> [!example] Example 1: $\mathbb{Q}_p$
> - Ring of integers: $\mathbb{Z}_p$
> - Uniformizer: $\pi = p$
> - Residue field: $\mathbb{F}_p$
> - $\mathbb{Q}_p^\times \cong p^\mathbb{Z} \times \mathbb{Z}_p^\times$

> [!example] Example 2: $\mathbb{Q}_p(\sqrt{p})$ (ramified)
> - Degree 2 over $\mathbb{Q}_p$
> - Uniformizer: $\pi = \sqrt{p}$
> - Residue field: $\mathbb{F}_p$ (unchanged)
> - Ramification index $e = 2$

> [!example] Example 3: $\mathbb{Q}_p(\zeta_{p-1})$ (unramified)
> - Degree $\varphi(p-1)$ over $\mathbb{Q}_p$
> - Uniformizer: $\pi = p$
> - Residue field: $\mathbb{F}_{p^{[K:\mathbb{Q}_p]}}$
> - Ramification index $e = 1$

> [!example] Example 4: $\mathbb{F}_p((t))$
> - Ring of integers: $\mathbb{F}_p[[t]]$
> - Uniformizer: $\pi = t$
> - Residue field: $\mathbb{F}_p$

## Extensions of Local Fields

### Ramification

For $L/K$ a finite extension of local fields:

| Invariant | Definition |
|-----------|------------|
| Ramification index | $e = v_L(\pi_K)$ |
| Inertia degree | $f = [k_L : k_K]$ |
| Fundamental relation | $[L:K] = ef$ |

### Types of Extensions

| Type | Condition | $e$ | $f$ |
|------|-----------|-----|-----|
| Unramified | $e = 1$ | 1 | $[L:K]$ |
| Totally ramified | $f = 1$ | $[L:K]$ | 1 |
| Tamely ramified | $p \nmid e$ | any | any |
| Wildly ramified | $p \mid e$ | $\geq p$ | any |

## Galois Theory of Local Fields

> [!abstract] Theorem (Structure of $\text{Gal}(\bar{K}/K)$)
> For $K$ a $p$-adic field:
> $$1 \to I_K \to G_K \to \text{Gal}(\bar{k}/k) \to 1$$
> where:
> - $I_K$ = inertia group (pro-finite, with wild inertia)
> - $\text{Gal}(\bar{k}/k) \cong \hat{\mathbb{Z}}$ generated by Frobenius

### Local Class Field Theory

> [!abstract] Theorem (Local Artin Map)
> There is a canonical isomorphism:
> $$K^\times \xrightarrow{\sim} G_K^{\text{ab}}$$
> sending uniformizers to (lifts of) Frobenius.

## Hensel's Lemma

> [!abstract] Theorem (Hensel's Lemma)
> Let $f(x) \in \mathcal{O}_K[x]$. If $a \in \mathcal{O}_K$ satisfies:
> $$v(f(a)) > 2v(f'(a))$$
> then there exists a unique $\alpha \in \mathcal{O}_K$ with $f(\alpha) = 0$ and $v(\alpha - a) > v(f'(a))$.

This allows lifting solutions from $k$ to $K$!

## Applications in Arithmetic Geometry

1. **Local-global principle**: Study $X(\mathbb{Q})$ via $X(\mathbb{Q}_p)$ for all $p$
2. **Galois representations**: $G_{\mathbb{Q}_p}$ acts on étale cohomology
3. **$p$-adic Hodge theory**: Relate $p$-adic and de Rham cohomology
4. **Reduction of varieties**: Study $X_{\mathbb{F}_p}$ via $X_{\mathbb{Q}_p}$
