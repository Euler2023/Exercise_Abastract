# Root Systems

## Definition

> [!info] Definition (Root System)
> A **root system** in a Euclidean space $E$ (finite-dimensional real inner product space) is a finite set $\Phi \subset E$ satisfying:
>
> 1. $\Phi$ spans $E$ and does not contain $0$
> 2. If $\alpha \in \Phi$, then $-\alpha \in \Phi$, and $\mathbb{R}\alpha \cap \Phi = \{\pm\alpha\}$
> 3. For each $\alpha \in \Phi$, the reflection $s_\alpha$ preserves $\Phi$
> 4. For $\alpha, \beta \in \Phi$: $\langle \beta, \alpha^\vee \rangle \in \mathbb{Z}$ where $\alpha^\vee = \frac{2\alpha}{\langle\alpha,\alpha\rangle}$

The integer $\langle \beta, \alpha^\vee \rangle$ is called a **Cartan integer**.

## Reflections and Weyl Group

> [!info] Definition (Reflection)
> The reflection through the hyperplane perpendicular to $\alpha$:
> $$s_\alpha(\beta) = \beta - \langle \beta, \alpha^\vee \rangle \alpha$$

> [!info] Definition (Weyl Group)
> The **Weyl group** $W$ is the group generated by all reflections $s_\alpha$ for $\alpha \in \Phi$.
>
> $W$ is always a finite group.

## Simple Roots and Positive Roots

> [!info] Definition
> Choose a hyperplane not containing any root. This divides $\Phi$ into:
> - **Positive roots** $\Phi^+$: roots on one side
> - **Negative roots** $\Phi^- = -\Phi^+$: roots on the other side
>
> **Simple roots** $\Delta = \{\alpha_1, \ldots, \alpha_n\}$: positive roots that cannot be written as a sum of other positive roots.

> [!abstract] Properties
> 1. $|\Delta| = \dim E = \text{rank}(\Phi)$
> 2. Every positive root is a non-negative integer combination of simple roots
> 3. Simple roots form a basis of $E$
> 4. $W$ is generated by simple reflections $s_{\alpha_i}$

## Cartan Matrix

> [!info] Definition
> The **Cartan matrix** $A = (a_{ij})$ has entries:
> $$a_{ij} = \langle \alpha_j, \alpha_i^\vee \rangle$$
>
> Properties:
> - $a_{ii} = 2$ (diagonal entries)
> - $a_{ij} \leq 0$ for $i \neq j$ (off-diagonal are non-positive)
> - $a_{ij} = 0 \Leftrightarrow a_{ji} = 0$

## Dynkin Diagrams

A Dynkin diagram encodes the Cartan matrix graphically:
- Nodes represent simple roots
- Edges encode angles between roots

| $a_{ij} \cdot a_{ji}$ | Angle | Diagram |
|----------------------|-------|---------|
| 0 | 90° | No edge |
| 1 | 120° | Single edge |
| 2 | 135° | Double edge with arrow |
| 3 | 150° | Triple edge with arrow |

The arrow points toward the shorter root.

## Classical Root Systems

### Type $A_{n-1}$: $\mathfrak{sl}_n$

$$\Phi = \{e_i - e_j : 1 \leq i \neq j \leq n\} \subset \mathbb{R}^n$$

- $|\Phi| = n(n-1)$
- Simple roots: $\alpha_i = e_i - e_{i+1}$ for $i = 1, \ldots, n-1$
- Weyl group: $W \cong S_n$ (symmetric group)

> [!example] $A_2$ ($\mathfrak{sl}_3$)
> Six roots forming a hexagon:
> $$\pm(e_1 - e_2), \pm(e_2 - e_3), \pm(e_1 - e_3)$$
> Dynkin diagram: $\circ - \circ$

### Type $B_n$: $\mathfrak{so}_{2n+1}$

$$\Phi = \{\pm e_i, \pm e_i \pm e_j : i \neq j\}$$

- $|\Phi| = 2n^2$
- Both long roots ($e_i \pm e_j$) and short roots ($e_i$)
- Weyl group: $W \cong (\mathbb{Z}/2)^n \rtimes S_n$

### Type $C_n$: $\mathfrak{sp}_{2n}$

$$\Phi = \{\pm 2e_i, \pm e_i \pm e_j : i \neq j\}$$

- $|\Phi| = 2n^2$
- Long roots ($2e_i$) and short roots ($e_i \pm e_j$)
- Dual to $B_n$

### Type $D_n$: $\mathfrak{so}_{2n}$

$$\Phi = \{\pm e_i \pm e_j : i \neq j\}$$

- $|\Phi| = 2n(n-1)$
- All roots have the same length
- Weyl group: $W \cong (\mathbb{Z}/2)^{n-1} \rtimes S_n$

## Classification Theorem

> [!abstract] Theorem (Classification)
> Every irreducible root system is isomorphic to one of:
> $$A_n \ (n \geq 1), \quad B_n \ (n \geq 2), \quad C_n \ (n \geq 3), \quad D_n \ (n \geq 4)$$
> $$E_6, \quad E_7, \quad E_8, \quad F_4, \quad G_2$$

## Connection to Lie Algebras

> [!abstract] Theorem
> For a semisimple Lie algebra $\mathfrak{g}$ with Cartan subalgebra $\mathfrak{h}$:
>
> The set of roots (nonzero weights of the adjoint representation on $\mathfrak{h}^*$) forms a root system.
>
> The classification of root systems corresponds to the classification of simple Lie algebras.

### Root Space Decomposition

$$\mathfrak{g} = \mathfrak{h} \oplus \bigoplus_{\alpha \in \Phi} \mathfrak{g}_\alpha$$

where $\mathfrak{g}_\alpha = \{X \in \mathfrak{g} : [H, X] = \alpha(H)X \text{ for all } H \in \mathfrak{h}\}$.

Each root space $\mathfrak{g}_\alpha$ is one-dimensional.

## Properties

> [!abstract] Key Facts
> 1. $[\mathfrak{g}_\alpha, \mathfrak{g}_\beta] = \mathfrak{g}_{\alpha+\beta}$ if $\alpha + \beta \in \Phi$
> 2. $[\mathfrak{g}_\alpha, \mathfrak{g}_\beta] = 0$ if $\alpha + \beta \neq 0$ and $\alpha + \beta \notin \Phi$
> 3. $[\mathfrak{g}_\alpha, \mathfrak{g}_{-\alpha}] \subset \mathfrak{h}$
> 4. The Killing form is non-degenerate on $\mathfrak{h}$ and $\mathfrak{g}_\alpha \oplus \mathfrak{g}_{-\alpha}$
