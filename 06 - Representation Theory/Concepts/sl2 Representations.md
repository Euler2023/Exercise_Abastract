# sl₂ Representations

## The Lie Algebra $\mathfrak{sl}_2$

> [!info] Definition
> $\mathfrak{sl}_2(\mathbb{C})$ is the Lie algebra of $2 \times 2$ traceless complex matrices.
>
> **Standard basis**:
> $$e = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix}, \quad f = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}, \quad h = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 \end{pmatrix}$$
>
> **Bracket relations**:
> $$[h, e] = 2e, \quad [h, f] = -2f, \quad [e, f] = h$$

## Finite-Dimensional Representations

> [!abstract] Classification Theorem
> Every finite-dimensional irreducible representation of $\mathfrak{sl}_2(\mathbb{C})$ is uniquely determined by its dimension.
>
> For each $n \geq 1$, there exists exactly one irreducible representation $V_n$ of dimension $n$.

### The Standard Representation ($V_2$)

The **defining representation** on $\mathbb{C}^2$:
$$\rho(e) = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix}, \quad \rho(f) = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}, \quad \rho(h) = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 \end{pmatrix}$$

### Weight Space Decomposition

> [!info] Definition (Weight)
> A **weight** of a representation $V$ is an eigenvalue of $h$:
> $$V_\lambda = \{v \in V : h \cdot v = \lambda v\}$$
> is the **weight space** for weight $\lambda$.

> [!abstract] Key Properties
> In any finite-dimensional representation:
> 1. $e$ raises the weight by 2: $e: V_\lambda \to V_{\lambda+2}$
> 2. $f$ lowers the weight by 2: $f: V_\lambda \to V_{\lambda-2}$
> 3. Weights are integers

### Structure of $V_n$

The irreducible representation $V_n$ has:
- **Dimension**: $n$
- **Weights**: $n-1, n-3, n-5, \ldots, -(n-3), -(n-1)$
- **Highest weight**: $n-1$
- **Basis**: $v_0, v_1, \ldots, v_{n-1}$ where $v_k = f^k \cdot v_0$

**Actions**:
$$h \cdot v_k = (n-1-2k) v_k$$
$$f \cdot v_k = v_{k+1} \quad (v_n = 0)$$
$$e \cdot v_k = k(n-k) v_{k-1} \quad (v_{-1} = 0)$$

## Examples

> [!example] Example 1: Trivial Representation ($V_1$)
> One-dimensional with $e, f, h$ all acting as zero.
> Weight: $0$.

> [!example] Example 2: Standard Representation ($V_2$)
> Two-dimensional on $\mathbb{C}^2$.
> Weights: $1, -1$.
> Basis: $v_0 = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix}$, $v_1 = \begin{pmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}$.

> [!example] Example 3: Adjoint Representation ($V_3$)
> Three-dimensional on $\mathfrak{sl}_2$ itself via $\text{ad}$.
> Weights: $2, 0, -2$.
> Basis: $e$ (weight 2), $h$ (weight 0), $f$ (weight -2).

> [!example] Example 4: Symmetric Powers ($V_{n+1} \cong \text{Sym}^n(V_2)$)
> The $(n+1)$-dimensional irreducible is isomorphic to $\text{Sym}^n(\mathbb{C}^2)$.
> Basis: $x^n, x^{n-1}y, \ldots, xy^{n-1}, y^n$ (homogeneous polynomials of degree $n$).

## Highest Weight Theory

> [!info] Definition
> A **highest weight vector** is $v \in V$ with:
> 1. $h \cdot v = \lambda v$ for some $\lambda$ (the highest weight)
> 2. $e \cdot v = 0$

> [!abstract] Theorem
> Every finite-dimensional irreducible representation has a unique (up to scalar) highest weight vector.
>
> The representation is generated by applying $f$ repeatedly to this vector.

### Construction

Given highest weight $\lambda \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}$:
1. Start with highest weight vector $v_0$: $e \cdot v_0 = 0$, $h \cdot v_0 = \lambda v_0$
2. Apply $f$ to get $v_1, v_2, \ldots$
3. The representation has dimension $\lambda + 1$

## Casimir Operator

> [!info] Definition
> The **Casimir element** of $\mathfrak{sl}_2$ is:
> $$\Omega = ef + fe + \frac{h^2}{2} = 2ef + h + \frac{h^2}{2} = 2fe - h + \frac{h^2}{2}$$

> [!abstract] Property
> $\Omega$ commutes with all of $\mathfrak{sl}_2$ and acts on $V_n$ by the scalar:
> $$\Omega|_{V_n} = \frac{(n-1)(n+1)}{2} = \frac{n^2 - 1}{2}$$

## Tensor Products

> [!abstract] Clebsch-Gordan Decomposition
> $$V_m \otimes V_n \cong V_{m+n-1} \oplus V_{m+n-3} \oplus \cdots \oplus V_{|m-n|+1}$$
>
> (All irreducibles from $m+n-1$ down to $|m-n|+1$ in steps of 2)

> [!example] Examples
> - $V_2 \otimes V_2 \cong V_3 \oplus V_1$ (spin $\frac{1}{2} \otimes \frac{1}{2} = 1 \oplus 0$)
> - $V_3 \otimes V_2 \cong V_4 \oplus V_2$
> - $V_3 \otimes V_3 \cong V_5 \oplus V_3 \oplus V_1$

## Connection to SU(2) and Physics

The complexification $\mathfrak{su}_2 \otimes \mathbb{C} \cong \mathfrak{sl}_2(\mathbb{C})$.

| Math notation | Physics notation |
|---------------|-----------------|
| $V_n$ | Spin $\frac{n-1}{2}$ |
| $h$ | $2J_z$ |
| $e$ | $J_+$ (raising) |
| $f$ | $J_-$ (lowering) |
| Weights | $2m$ where $m$ is magnetic quantum number |
