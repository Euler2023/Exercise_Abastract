# Exercise Rep7: Root System of sl₃

## Problem Statement

> [!question] Problem
> Consider $\mathfrak{sl}_3(\mathbb{C})$, the Lie algebra of traceless $3 \times 3$ complex matrices.
>
> **(a)** Show that the diagonal matrices in $\mathfrak{sl}_3$ form a Cartan subalgebra $\mathfrak{h}$. What is $\dim \mathfrak{h}$?
>
> **(b)** For each $i \neq j$, let $E_{ij}$ be the matrix with 1 in position $(i,j)$ and 0 elsewhere. Show that $E_{ij}$ is a root vector and find its root $\alpha_{ij} \in \mathfrak{h}^*$.
>
> **(c)** Find all 6 roots and draw the root diagram in $\mathfrak{h}^*_\mathbb{R}$ (a 2-dimensional real vector space).
>
> **(d)** Identify the simple roots $\alpha, \beta$ and compute the Cartan matrix $A = \begin{pmatrix} \langle\alpha, \alpha^\vee\rangle & \langle\beta, \alpha^\vee\rangle \\ \langle\alpha, \beta^\vee\rangle & \langle\beta, \beta^\vee\rangle \end{pmatrix}$.
>
> **(e)** Describe the Weyl group $W$ of this root system and show $|W| = 6$.

## Hints

> [!hint]- Hint 1 (Part a)
> A Cartan subalgebra is maximal abelian consisting of semisimple elements. Diagonal matrices commute.

> [!hint]- Hint 2 (Part b)
> Compute $[H, E_{ij}]$ for diagonal $H = \text{diag}(h_1, h_2, h_3)$ with $h_1 + h_2 + h_3 = 0$.

> [!hint]- Hint 3 (Part c)
> The roots are $\varepsilon_i - \varepsilon_j$ where $\varepsilon_i(H) = h_i$. But these satisfy $\varepsilon_1 + \varepsilon_2 + \varepsilon_3 = 0$ on $\mathfrak{h}$.

> [!hint]- Hint 4 (Part d)
> Choose $\alpha = \varepsilon_1 - \varepsilon_2$ and $\beta = \varepsilon_2 - \varepsilon_3$ as simple roots.

> [!hint]- Hint 5 (Part e)
> The Weyl group is generated by reflections $s_\alpha$ and $s_\beta$. It permutes the roots.

## Solution

> [!success]- Solution
>
> **(a) Cartan subalgebra**
>
> Let $\mathfrak{h} = \{H = \text{diag}(h_1, h_2, h_3) : h_1 + h_2 + h_3 = 0\}$.
>
> - **Abelian**: Diagonal matrices commute.
> - **Maximal**: Any matrix commuting with all diagonal matrices must be diagonal.
> - **Semisimple**: Diagonal matrices are semisimple (diagonalizable).
>
> $\dim \mathfrak{h} = 2$ (3 diagonal entries with one constraint).
>
> ---
>
> **(b) Root vectors**
>
> Let $H = \text{diag}(h_1, h_2, h_3) \in \mathfrak{h}$.
>
> $$[H, E_{ij}] = HE_{ij} - E_{ij}H$$
>
> Since $HE_{ij}$ has $h_i$ in position $(i,j)$ and $E_{ij}H$ has $h_j$ in position $(i,j)$:
> $$[H, E_{ij}] = (h_i - h_j)E_{ij}$$
>
> So $E_{ij}$ is a root vector with root $\alpha_{ij}$ defined by:
> $$\alpha_{ij}(H) = h_i - h_j$$
>
> In functional notation: $\alpha_{ij} = \varepsilon_i - \varepsilon_j$ where $\varepsilon_i(H) = h_i$.
>
> ---
>
> **(c) The 6 roots**
>
> $$\Phi = \{\varepsilon_1 - \varepsilon_2, \varepsilon_2 - \varepsilon_3, \varepsilon_1 - \varepsilon_3, \varepsilon_2 - \varepsilon_1, \varepsilon_3 - \varepsilon_2, \varepsilon_3 - \varepsilon_1\}$$
>
> Or in pairs: $\pm(\varepsilon_1 - \varepsilon_2), \pm(\varepsilon_2 - \varepsilon_3), \pm(\varepsilon_1 - \varepsilon_3)$.
>
> **Root diagram:** These form a regular hexagon in the plane $\varepsilon_1 + \varepsilon_2 + \varepsilon_3 = 0$:
>
> ```
>           ε₁-ε₃
>          /    \
>    ε₁-ε₂        ε₂-ε₃
>        •    0    •
>    ε₂-ε₁        ε₃-ε₂
>          \    /
>           ε₃-ε₁
> ```
>
> This is the $A_2$ root system.
>
> ---
>
> **(d) Simple roots and Cartan matrix**
>
> **Positive roots** (choosing $h_1 > h_2 > h_3$):
> $$\Phi^+ = \{\varepsilon_1 - \varepsilon_2, \varepsilon_2 - \varepsilon_3, \varepsilon_1 - \varepsilon_3\}$$
>
> **Simple roots** (positive roots not sums of other positive roots):
> $$\alpha = \varepsilon_1 - \varepsilon_2, \quad \beta = \varepsilon_2 - \varepsilon_3$$
>
> Note: $\varepsilon_1 - \varepsilon_3 = \alpha + \beta$ (not simple).
>
> **Inner product:** Using the Killing form restricted to $\mathfrak{h}$:
> $$\langle \varepsilon_i - \varepsilon_j, \varepsilon_k - \varepsilon_\ell \rangle = \delta_{ik} - \delta_{i\ell} - \delta_{jk} + \delta_{j\ell}$$
>
> So:
> - $\langle \alpha, \alpha \rangle = 2$
> - $\langle \beta, \beta \rangle = 2$
> - $\langle \alpha, \beta \rangle = -1$
>
> **Cartan integers:**
> $$\langle \alpha, \alpha^\vee \rangle = 2, \quad \langle \beta, \alpha^\vee \rangle = \frac{2\langle\beta,\alpha\rangle}{\langle\alpha,\alpha\rangle} = \frac{2(-1)}{2} = -1$$
> $$\langle \alpha, \beta^\vee \rangle = -1, \quad \langle \beta, \beta^\vee \rangle = 2$$
>
> **Cartan matrix:**
> $$A = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}$$
>
> This is the Cartan matrix of type $A_2$.
>
> ---
>
> **(e) Weyl group**
>
> The Weyl group is generated by reflections:
> $$s_\alpha(\lambda) = \lambda - \langle\lambda, \alpha^\vee\rangle \alpha$$
> $$s_\beta(\lambda) = \lambda - \langle\lambda, \beta^\vee\rangle \beta$$
>
> **Action on roots:**
> - $s_\alpha$: swaps $\varepsilon_1 \leftrightarrow \varepsilon_2$
> - $s_\beta$: swaps $\varepsilon_2 \leftrightarrow \varepsilon_3$
>
> These generate all permutations of $\{\varepsilon_1, \varepsilon_2, \varepsilon_3\}$, so:
> $$W \cong S_3$$
>
> Therefore $|W| = 6$.
>
> Elements: $\{1, s_\alpha, s_\beta, s_\alpha s_\beta, s_\beta s_\alpha, s_\alpha s_\beta s_\alpha\}$.
