# Exercise MF1: Verifying the Modularity of E₄

## Problem Statement

> [!question] Problem
> The normalized Eisenstein series of weight 4 is defined as:
> $$E_4(\tau) = 1 + 240\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \sigma_3(n) q^n$$
> where $q = e^{2\pi i \tau}$ and $\sigma_3(n) = \sum_{d|n} d^3$.
>
> **(a)** Compute the first five terms of the $q$-expansion of $E_4(\tau)$, i.e., find $a_0, a_1, a_2, a_3, a_4$ where $E_4 = \sum a_n q^n$.
>
> **(b)** Verify that $E_4(\tau + 1) = E_4(\tau)$.
>
> **(c)** Explain why the periodicity in (b) is necessary for $E_4$ to be a modular form.

## Hints

> [!hint]- Hint 1
> For part (a), you need to compute $\sigma_3(n)$ for $n = 1, 2, 3, 4$. Remember that $\sigma_3(n) = \sum_{d|n} d^3$.

> [!hint]- Hint 2
> For part (b), recall that $q = e^{2\pi i \tau}$. What happens to $q$ when you replace $\tau$ with $\tau + 1$?

> [!hint]- Hint 3
> For part (c), think about the generator $T = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix}$ of the modular group and its action on $\tau$.

## Solution

> [!success]- Solution
> **(a)** Computing divisor sums:
> - $\sigma_3(1) = 1^3 = 1$
> - $\sigma_3(2) = 1^3 + 2^3 = 1 + 8 = 9$
> - $\sigma_3(3) = 1^3 + 3^3 = 1 + 27 = 28$
> - $\sigma_3(4) = 1^3 + 2^3 + 4^3 = 1 + 8 + 64 = 73$
>
> Therefore:
> $$E_4(\tau) = 1 + 240(1)q + 240(9)q^2 + 240(28)q^3 + 240(73)q^4 + \cdots$$
> $$= 1 + 240q + 2160q^2 + 6720q^3 + 17520q^4 + \cdots$$
>
> So $a_0 = 1$, $a_1 = 240$, $a_2 = 2160$, $a_3 = 6720$, $a_4 = 17520$.
>
> **(b)** When $\tau \mapsto \tau + 1$:
> $$q = e^{2\pi i \tau} \mapsto e^{2\pi i (\tau + 1)} = e^{2\pi i \tau} \cdot e^{2\pi i} = q \cdot 1 = q$$
>
> Since $q$ is unchanged, and $E_4(\tau)$ depends only on $q$, we have $E_4(\tau + 1) = E_4(\tau)$.
>
> **(c)** The modular group $\text{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})$ is generated by $S$ and $T$ where $T: \tau \mapsto \tau + 1$. For $E_4$ to be a modular form, it must satisfy $E_4(\gamma \cdot \tau) = (c\tau + d)^4 E_4(\tau)$ for all $\gamma \in \text{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})$. In particular, for $T$:
> $$E_4(T \cdot \tau) = E_4(\tau + 1) = (0 \cdot \tau + 1)^4 E_4(\tau) = E_4(\tau)$$
>
> So periodicity under $\tau \mapsto \tau + 1$ is the modularity condition for the generator $T$.
