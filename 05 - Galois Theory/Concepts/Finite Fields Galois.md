# Galois Theory of Finite Fields

Finite fields have a particularly elegant Galois theory: all extensions are cyclic and generated by the Frobenius automorphism.

## Structure of Finite Field Extensions

> [!info] Basic Setup
> - Base field: $\mathbb{F}_p$ (prime field with $p$ elements)
> - Extension: $\mathbb{F}_{p^n}$ (unique field of order $p^n$)
> - The extension $\mathbb{F}_{p^n}/\mathbb{F}_p$ is always Galois

## The Frobenius Automorphism

> [!info] Definition (Frobenius)
> The **Frobenius automorphism** $\phi: \mathbb{F}_{p^n} \to \mathbb{F}_{p^n}$ is defined by:
> $$\phi(x) = x^p$$
>
> This is an $\mathbb{F}_p$-automorphism (fixes $\mathbb{F}_p$ pointwise).

> [!abstract] Key Properties of Frobenius
> 1. $\phi$ is a ring homomorphism: $\phi(x+y) = \phi(x) + \phi(y)$, $\phi(xy) = \phi(x)\phi(y)$
> 2. $\phi$ fixes exactly $\mathbb{F}_p$: $\phi(x) = x \iff x \in \mathbb{F}_p$
> 3. $\phi$ has order $n$ in $\text{Gal}(\mathbb{F}_{p^n}/\mathbb{F}_p)$
> 4. $\phi^n = \text{id}$ (since $x^{p^n} = x$ for all $x \in \mathbb{F}_{p^n}$)

## Galois Group of Finite Fields

> [!abstract] Main Theorem
> $$\text{Gal}(\mathbb{F}_{p^n}/\mathbb{F}_p) = \langle \phi \rangle \cong \mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}$$
>
> The Galois group is cyclic of order $n$, generated by Frobenius.

## Galois Correspondence for Finite Fields

> [!info] Subfields and Subgroups
> For $\mathbb{F}_{p^n}/\mathbb{F}_p$:
>
> | Divisor $d$ of $n$ | Subgroup | Subfield |
> |-------------------|----------|----------|
> | $d$ | $\langle \phi^{n/d} \rangle$ (order $d$) | $\mathbb{F}_{p^{n/d}}$ |
>
> The subfields of $\mathbb{F}_{p^n}$ are exactly $\mathbb{F}_{p^d}$ where $d | n$.

## Examples

> [!example] Example 1: $\mathbb{F}_{p^2}/\mathbb{F}_p$
> - $\text{Gal} = \{id, \phi\} \cong \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$
> - $\phi(x) = x^p$ (the only non-identity automorphism)
> - No proper intermediate fields

> [!example] Example 2: $\mathbb{F}_{p^6}/\mathbb{F}_p$
> Divisors of 6: 1, 2, 3, 6
>
> | Subgroup | Generator | Fixed Field |
> |----------|-----------|-------------|
> | $\langle \phi^6 \rangle = \{1\}$ | - | $\mathbb{F}_{p^6}$ |
> | $\langle \phi^3 \rangle$ (order 2) | $\phi^3$ | $\mathbb{F}_{p^3}$ |
> | $\langle \phi^2 \rangle$ (order 3) | $\phi^2$ | $\mathbb{F}_{p^2}$ |
> | $\langle \phi \rangle$ (order 6) | $\phi$ | $\mathbb{F}_p$ |

> [!example] Example 3: $\mathbb{F}_{2^4}/\mathbb{F}_2$
> - $\text{Gal} \cong \mathbb{Z}/4\mathbb{Z}$
> - Subfields: $\mathbb{F}_2 \subset \mathbb{F}_4 \subset \mathbb{F}_{16}$
> - $\mathbb{F}_4 = \{x \in \mathbb{F}_{16} : x^4 = x\}$

## The Norm and Trace

> [!info] Definition
> For $\alpha \in \mathbb{F}_{p^n}$:
>
> **Trace**: $\text{Tr}(\alpha) = \alpha + \alpha^p + \alpha^{p^2} + \cdots + \alpha^{p^{n-1}}$
>
> **Norm**: $N(\alpha) = \alpha \cdot \alpha^p \cdot \alpha^{p^2} \cdots \alpha^{p^{n-1}} = \alpha^{(p^n-1)/(p-1)}$
>
> Both map $\mathbb{F}_{p^n} \to \mathbb{F}_p$.

## Applications

> [!tip] Uses of Finite Field Galois Theory
> 1. **Counting subfields**: Subfields of $\mathbb{F}_{p^n}$ â†” divisors of $n$
> 2. **Irreducibility testing**: Use Frobenius orbits
> 3. **Coding theory**: Cyclic codes, BCH codes
> 4. **Cryptography**: Elliptic curves over finite fields

## Relative Extensions

> [!abstract] General Case
> For $\mathbb{F}_{p^n}/\mathbb{F}_{p^m}$ where $m | n$:
> $$\text{Gal}(\mathbb{F}_{p^n}/\mathbb{F}_{p^m}) = \langle \phi^m \rangle \cong \mathbb{Z}/(n/m)\mathbb{Z}$$
>
> Generated by the "relative Frobenius" $x \mapsto x^{p^m}$.
